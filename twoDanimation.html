What is 2D Animation?

2D animation refers to the creation of moving images in a two-dimensional space.
2D animation focuses on characters, objects, and backgrounds that exist within a flat, two-dimensional plane.

There are various steps we need to follow to make 2D or Traditional animation.
This steps are following:

Idea and Storyboarding:

Conceptualization: Define the concept and purpose of your animation. What story or message do you want to convey?

Storyboarding: Create a sequence of rough sketches or frames that outline the key scenes and actions in your animation. This serves as a visual script.

Character Design and Asset Creation:

Character Design: If your animation involves characters, design and create their visual appearance, considering their personalities and roles in the story.

Asset Creation: Create any additional visual elements, backgrounds, or objects needed for the animation.

Audio and Voiceovers:

Script: If your animation includes dialogue, write a script for the characters

Voiceovers: Record or hire voice actors to provide voices for the characters. This step can be done before or after animation, depending on your workflow.

Animatic:

Animatic Creation: Develop a rough version of your animation by putting together the storyboard frames, adding the voiceovers, and timing the sequence. This helps to visualize the pacing and flow of the animation.

Layout and Backgrounds:

Layout: Plan the placement of characters and objects in each frame.

Backgrounds: Create or design the backgrounds for each scene

Animation:

Traditional Animation: If you're working with traditional hand-drawn animation, create each frame by hand, often on paper or using a digital drawing tablet. Digital Animation: Use animation software (e.g., Adobe Animate, Toon Boom Harmony) to create and manipulate digital images for each frame.

In-betweening:

Traditional Animation: If frames are not drawn for every animation pose, create in-between frames to ensure smooth motion.

Digital Animation: Many animation software tools have automated tweening functions to generate in-between frames.

Coloring and Texturing:

Traditional Animation: Add colors to each frame using traditional art supplies.

Digital Animation: Use digital coloring tools to add colors and textures to your animated elements.

Finalizing:

Editing: Assemble all the frames into a cohesive sequence, considering the timing and transitions between frames. Adding Effects: Add any special effects or additional elements to enhance the animation.

Export and Distribution:

Export: Export the final animation in a suitable format (common formats include GIF, MP4, or AVI).

Distribution: Share your animation through various platforms such as websites, social media, or film festivals.
